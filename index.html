What is it?

Ibanez released a new version of the RG670AH solid body electric guitar model in 2013. It is a component of the Genesis Collection, a set of RG and Artist reissues that was only available in Japan. Based on the original RG670AH, which was created in the middle of the 1990s, this model is a reprint.

Ash body with maple and walnut Super Wizard neck and 24-fret rosewood fingerboard with dot position markers make up the RG670AH. The body and neck are connected by a conventional bolt on joint with a metal plate; but, in this instance, the plate has been slanted to make it simpler to reach the top frets. Edge double locking tremolo bridge, locking nut, Gotoh tuners, and HSH-configured Ibanez pickups are among the parts.

My Experience

One of my favorite part about this guitar is its thin neck, making it easy to play fast solos and complex riffs. The jumbo frets also make it easier to play with precision

The Ibanez RG670AH also features a locking tremolo system, which allows for precise tuning and stable performance even with heavy use of the tremolo bar. The hardware on the guitar is of high quality and feels solid, which adds to the overall playing experience.

Overall, I think the Ibanez RG670AH is a superb guitar for players who want a fast-playing, versatile instrument that can handle a wide range of musical styles.
